Autumn and Winter Festivals

Andong International Mask Dance Festival

  • Location: Andong, Gyeongsangbuk-do
  • Traditional features: a global festival that showcases Korean traditional folk art, 'mask dance', and various international folk performances. Traditional mask dance, street parade, folk performance, experience booths are included.
  • Festival dates: late September to early October
  • Visiting tips: 1 check the performance schedule 2 walking or using public transportation is convenient as the festival venues are in the downtown area. 3 bring a camera since there are many photos and cultural experience opportunities.

References: Andong Maskdance Festival (Official Site)

Jinju Namgang Lantern Festival

  • Location: Jinju, Gyeongsangnam-do
  • Historical background: originated from the Battle of Jinju during the Imjin War. It started with floating lanterns for military signaling and consolation. Nowadays, the festival symbolizes fallen patriots and hopes for peace.
  • Festival dates: early to mid-October
  • Visiting tips: 1 bring a jacket as it is a night-time festival 2 bring a camera since the night scenery is beautiful. 3 check the schedule for lantern floating events, parades, and exhibitions.

References: Jinju Namgang Lantern Festival (Official Site)

Hwacheon Sancheoneo Ice Festival

  • Location: Hwacheon-eup, Hwacheon-gun, Gangwon-do
  • Key highlights: ice fishing, hand-catching, winter leisure activities, cooking or enjoying the fish raw
  • Festival dates: mid-January to early February
  • Visiting tips: 1 wear thick winter clothing, waterproof non-slip boots, gloves, hats, neck warmers, etc. 2 prepare towels and extra clothes 3 visit during daytime as it is safer. 4 check whether reservations are required.

References: Hwacheon Sancheoneo Ice Festival (Official Site)

Taebaeksan Snow Festival

  • Location: Taebaek, Gangwon-do
  • Key highlights: snow sculpture exhibition, snow sculpture contest, winter leisure activities, night illumination.
  • Festival dates: late January to early February
  • Visiting tips: bring jacket, thick gloves, scarf, winter boots, insulated tumbler, hand warmers.

References: Taebaeksan Snow Festival (Official Site)
