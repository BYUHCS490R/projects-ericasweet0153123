Korean Festival Culture

Seasonal and nature-focused features

Korean festivals are closely linked to the changing seasons. Spring flowers, summer seas and water activities, autumn harvests, and snow and ice in winter are reflected in these festivals.

Local culture and specialties

Most festivals are intended to promote the region's history, traditions, food, and specialties. For example, the Jinju Namgang Lantern Festival is based on local history, and the Boryeong Mud Festival makes use of unique local natural resources.

Hands-on programs

In addition to events that people watch, there are many interactive, experience-based programs. Making local food, wearing Hanbok (traditional Korean clothing), and similar activities allow both residents and visitors actively participate in the culture.
